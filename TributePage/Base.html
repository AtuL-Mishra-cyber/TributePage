10 interesting facts about Bose

  • Subhas Chandra Bose was born on January 23, 1897, in Cuttack, Odisha, India. He came from a well-educated family and excelled in academics from a young age.
  • Bose passed the prestigious Indian Civil Services examination but resigned from his job in 1921 to join the freedom movement led by Mahatma Gandhi.
  • Bose traveled to England to study at Cambridge University, but he was drawn to nationalism and left for the United States. He attended Harvard University and earned a degree in Philosophy.
  • Bose became a prominent leader of the Indian National Congress and later became the President of the party in 1938 and 1939.
  • Subhas Chandra Bose's legacy as a charismatic leader, his contribution to India's freedom struggle, and his dedication to achieving independence have made him a revered figure in Indian history. His birthday, January 23rd, is celebrated as "Parakram Diwas" (Day of Valour) in his honor.
  • Dissatisfied with the nonviolent approach of the Indian National Congress, Bose established the Forward Bloc within the Congress, advocating for a more radical approach towards achieving India's freedom.
  • During World War II, Bose believed that India's struggle for independence could be aided by collaborating with the Axis powers. He escaped from house arrest and traveled to Germany and then Japan to seek their support.
  • Bose founded the Indian National Army (INA) with the support of Japanese authorities. The INA aimed to liberate India from British rule with military force.
  • "Jai Hind" Slogan: Bose coined the famous slogan "Jai Hind," which became a rallying cry for the INA and remains an important patriotic phrase in India.
  • ose's death in a plane crash in 1945 remains controversial and mysterious. Some believe he died in the crash, while others think he survived and went into hiding.
